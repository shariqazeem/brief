# Brief — submission narrative

> Adopt an operator. The chain holds the leash.

The whole product reduces to one differentiated claim: **most AI agents can spend
your money; Brief is the one where the chain controls the agent.** Everything
below serves that line — don't add anything that dilutes it.

---

## 1. The 2–3 minute demo script

**Hook (15s)**
> "Most AI agents can spend your money. Brief is the first one where the *chain*
> controls the agent — it can trade, but it physically cannot withdraw, overspend,
> or break your mandate, because Move enforces it."

**Adopt — owner-first (30s)** — `/workforce/adopt`
> "I don't configure a bot. I adopt a *goal*: Protect my capital, Grow steadily,
> or Beat passive SUI. I set the leash — a budget cap and an optional drawdown
> mandate. I deposit. One signature: it creates my BalanceManager (I own it),
> delegates a trade-only TradeCap, and writes an on-chain OperatorPolicy."

**Operator — it works for you (40s)** — `/workforce`
> "This is Halcyon. The page leads with *my objective* and whether it's beating
> the alternative — not with the bot. It marks my capital to market live, shows
> where the money lives (cash ↔ SUI), and its performance vs simply holding SUI."
- Point at: **Objective** card → **Performance** (vs Passive SUI vs Cash) →
  **Operator ledger** (decision → action → outcome) → **Protected by Sui** (the
  custody chain).

**Brain — watch it think (30s)** — `/brain`
> "Every decision is replayable, like a black box: what it saw, what it
> remembered, what it feared, what it did, what happened. Reasoning is anchored
> on Walrus — verifiable, not claimed."

**Results — did it work? (20s)** — `/results`
> "The bottom line: return vs holding vs cash, max drawdown, capital preserved,
> trades made and avoided, zero policy violations. Reliability is already solved."

**Proof — the moat (20s)** — `/proof`
> "And here's why you can trust it: I revoke, and the next time the operator
> tries to trade, the Move contract *aborts* the transaction on-chain. Not a
> setting it respects — a rule it cannot break."

**Close (15s)**
> "AI doesn't need more freedom. It needs constraints. Brief turns autonomous
> finance into something people can actually trust — and the operator is the
> product, finance is just the first vertical."

## 2. Mainnet story (the "when?" answer)

Reliability is the hard part, and it's done on testnet. The path is narrative, not
a promise of dates:

- **Now — Testnet validation.** Live operators, real DeepBook v3 orders,
  self-enforced budgets, on-chain revocation — running continuously with **zero
  policy violations and zero custody incidents.**
- **Next — Limited mainnet.** Invite a handful of operators with capped budgets
  and real USDC. The exact same architecture — nothing custodial changes.
- **Then — Public operator workforce.** Open adoption across objectives, and
  beyond finance.

The point for judges: *we already solved reliability; scaling capital is the next
step, not the risky one.* (This is surfaced live on `/results`.)

---

## 3. Company vision

Brief is **not** "a crypto trading agent." That framing is too small.

> **Brief builds autonomous operators** — agents that act in the world under
> constraints the owner sets and the chain enforces.

- **Today:** Halcyon — a finance operator (cash ↔ SUI on DeepBook).
- **Tomorrow:** Treasury operator · Yield operator · Stablecoin operator ·
  Creator operator · Commerce operator.

**The operator is the product. Finance is the first vertical.** The moat is the
same everywhere: non-custodial execution + Move-enforced constraints + verifiable,
revocable autonomy. (Company framing: Brief is Kyvernlabs' Sui product.)

---

## 4. What NOT to build (protect the thesis)

No chat interface. No AI copilot. No marketplace. No social feed. No DAO. No
token. No governance. Every new feature now risks lowering quality. Remaining
work before submission is **demo polish, copy, landing, video, screenshots,
launch/X positioning** — not engineering.
